The Red Panda

The red panda is a small, tree-dwelling mammal native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Despite its name, the red panda is not closely related to the giant panda. It has reddish-brown fur, a long bushy tail, and a waddling gait due to its shorter front legs. Red pandas are primarily herbivores, feeding on bamboo, but they also eat fruits, acorns, and insects. Known for their agility, they use their sharp claws to climb trees and escape predators. Unfortunately, red pandas are classified as endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.
